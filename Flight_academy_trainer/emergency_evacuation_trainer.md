## Aircraft Emergency Evacuation — Picture Description Practice



This picture shows a British Airways aircraft after an emergency.

The aircraft has suffered an engine fire.

The passengers have evacuated via the emergency chutes.

They are now calmly moving away from the aircraft.

This is very important because after an engine fire there may still be smoke, fire, fuel leakage, or a risk of explosion.

In this situation, passengers should follow crew instructions.

They should leave their baggage behind.

They should evacuate quickly and move to a safe distance.

The cabin crew should control the evacuation and help passengers leave the aircraft safely.

Emergency services should approach the aircraft and deal with the fire.

The main risk is that the fire may spread or the smoke may become dangerous.

That is why passengers must not stay close to the aircraft.

They should move upwind if possible and wait in a safe area.

The safest action is to evacuate quickly, remain calm, and move away from the aircraft.

Safety depends on fast evacuation, clear crew instructions, and good coordination with emergency services.
