## 01 — No landing gear / engine ripped off
- Files: `01-no-landing-gear-text.png` + `01-no-landing-gear-photo.png`
- Card text: *AC without landing gear / bogged down / ripped of engine*

**ICAO description:**
In this picture I can see a twin-engine turboprop aircraft that has overrun the runway. The aircraft
has come to rest off the paved surface, with its nose down on a rocky bank beside a stretch of water.
The landing gear has collapsed, so the fuselage is resting directly on the ground, and one of the
engines has been ripped off the wing on impact. It looks like the crew either landed gear-up or the
gear failed during a runway excursion. Emergency responders and several people are standing around the
wreckage. Fortunately the aircraft is intact and there is no fire, so the passengers were probably
evacuated safely.

**ICAO Level 5 upgrade:**
Looking at the damage, it must have been a runway excursion after a high-speed landing. It could have
been caused by a landing-gear failure, or it might have been caused by the crew touching down too far
along the runway. The loss of an engine may indicate a very heavy impact with the rocks. From a safety
perspective, the priority is to evacuate the passengers and shut down the fuel system, because a
ruptured tank could have led to a post-crash fire.

**Repeat-After-Me ICAO Drill:**
1. "It must have been a runway excursion after landing."
2. "It might have been caused by a landing-gear failure."
3. "From a safety perspective, the crew should evacuate immediately."
4. "A ruptured fuel tank could have led to a fire."

**Mini Speaking Task:**
- Question: What do you think caused this accident, and what is the main risk now?
- Exam-safe answer: It could have been a gear-up landing or a runway overrun. The aircraft has lost an
  engine and the landing gear has collapsed. From a safety perspective, the main risk is a fuel fire,
  so the safest decision would be to evacuate the passengers and let the fire service secure the
  aircraft.
