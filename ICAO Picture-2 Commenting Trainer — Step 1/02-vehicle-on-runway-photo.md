## 02 — Airport vehicle on the runway / aircraft approaching
- Files: `02-vehicle-on-runway-text.png` + `02-vehicle-on-runway-photo.png`
- Card text: *airport vehicle / RW / approaching AC*

**ICAO description:**
This picture shows a serious runway incursion. A light green airport vehicle is crossing the runway
while a passenger jet is on short final, only a few metres above the threshold and about to touch down.
The vehicle is right in the touchdown zone, directly in the path of the landing aircraft. This is a
very dangerous situation that could lead to a collision. Most likely the driver entered the runway
without clearance, or there was a loss of communication with the tower. The controller should instruct
the aircraft to go around immediately and order the vehicle to vacate the runway.

**ICAO Level 5 upgrade:**
It must have been a runway incursion. It could have been caused by the driver entering the runway
without clearance, or it may have been caused by a misunderstanding of the controller's instruction.
The position of the vehicle in the touchdown zone may indicate that the driver was not aware of the
landing traffic. From a safety standpoint, this could have led to a catastrophic collision, so the
safest decision would be to send the aircraft around without delay.

**Repeat-After-Me ICAO Drill:**
1. "It must have been a runway incursion."
2. "It could have been caused by entering the runway without clearance."
3. "This could have led to a collision on the runway."
4. "From a safety standpoint, the aircraft should go around immediately."

**Mini Speaking Task:**
- Question: How serious is this situation, and what action would you take?
- Exam-safe answer: This is extremely serious — a vehicle is on the runway while the aircraft is about
  to land. The main risk is a collision in the touchdown zone. For safety reasons, the controller
  should order an immediate go-around and instruct the vehicle to vacate the runway at once.
