## 03 — Airport vehicle hit the aircraft on the ground
- Files: `03-vehicle-hit-aircraft-text.png` + `03-vehicle-hit-aircraft-photo.png`
- Card text: *airport vehicle / hit / AC on the ground*

**ICAO description:**
In this picture a ground service vehicle — it looks like a fuel or utility truck — has collided with a
parked aircraft on the apron. The truck has struck the nose section near the radome and the forward
fuselage, and you can see damage on the lower part of the nose. The aircraft was stationary at the
stand when the impact happened. This kind of ground-handling accident usually occurs when the driver is
manoeuvring too close to the aircraft or fails to keep a safe distance. The aircraft will have to be
inspected for structural damage before it can return to service.

**ICAO Level 5 upgrade:**
It must have been a ground-handling accident on the apron. It could have been caused by the driver
manoeuvring too fast, or it might have been caused by poor visibility or a lack of a marshaller. The
damage to the radome may indicate a hidden crack in the nose structure. From a safety perspective, the
aircraft cannot be released until it is fully inspected, because undetected damage could have led to a
structural failure in flight.
