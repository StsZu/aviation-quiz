## 04 — Aircraft on the ground, crippled, covered with foam
- Files: `04-foam-covered-text.png` + `04-foam-covered-photo.png`
- Card text: *AC on the ground / crippled / covered with foam*

**ICAO description:**
This picture shows the aftermath of an emergency landing. A passenger aircraft is sitting on the ground
and it is crippled — the fuselage is broken and the aircraft has been badly damaged. Airport fire
crews are spraying it with fire-fighting foam to prevent a fuel fire, and the aircraft and the apron
around it are covered with white foam. Firefighters and rescue vehicles are working around the wreck.
It looks like the aircraft suffered a gear failure or a hard landing and the crew evacuated the
passengers. The quick response of the fire service has prevented the fire from spreading.

**ICAO Level 5 upgrade:**
It must have been an emergency landing. It could have been caused by a landing-gear malfunction, or it
may have been caused by a hard touchdown that broke the fuselage. The amount of foam may indicate that
the crew were worried about a fuel leak and a possible fire. From a safety standpoint, covering the
aircraft with foam was the right action, because spilled fuel could have led to a major blaze.

**Repeat-After-Me ICAO Drill:**
1. "It must have been an emergency landing."
2. "It could have been caused by a landing-gear failure."
3. "The foam may indicate a risk of a fuel fire."
4. "From a safety standpoint, the foam prevents the fuel from igniting."

**Mini Speaking Task:**
- Question: Why is the aircraft covered with foam, and was it the right decision?
- Exam-safe answer: The aircraft is crippled after what must have been an emergency landing, and the
  fire crews have covered it with foam. The main risk is a fuel fire. For safety reasons, foaming the
  aircraft was the safest decision, because leaking fuel could have led to a serious blaze.
