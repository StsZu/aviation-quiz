## 05 — Aircraft with a big hole / lightning strike
- Files: `05-lightning-strike-text.png` + `05-lightning-strike-photo.png`
- Card text: *AC / big hole / lightning strike*

**ICAO description:**
In this picture I can see the nose of a regional jet that has been damaged in flight. There is a big
hole punched in the radome, just above the cockpit windows, and the skin around it is torn and dented.
This kind of damage is typical of a lightning strike or a bird strike during climb or descent. The
crew most likely felt an impact and noticed the damage after landing. Even though the hole looks
dramatic, the aircraft managed to land safely. The radome and the nose structure will have to be
repaired and the systems checked before the next flight.

**ICAO Level 5 upgrade:**
It must have been a strike that damaged the nose in flight. It could have been a lightning strike, or
it may have been a bird strike during climb or descent. The hole in the radome may indicate damage to
the weather radar behind it. From a safety perspective, the crew were right to land as soon as
possible, because a weakened nose structure could have led to further damage at high speed.

**Repeat-After-Me ICAO Drill:**
1. "It could have been a lightning strike."
2. "It may have been a bird strike during the descent."
3. "The hole may indicate damage to the weather radar."
4. "From a safety perspective, the crew should land as soon as possible."

**Mini Speaking Task:**
- Question: What could have made this hole, and how dangerous is it?
- Exam-safe answer: It could have been a lightning strike or a bird strike that punched a hole in the
  radome. It may indicate damage to the radar and the nose structure. From a safety standpoint, the
  defect cannot be ignored, so the nose must be repaired and the systems checked before the next
  flight.
