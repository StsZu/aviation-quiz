## 06 — Flock of birds approaching a jet aircraft
- Files: `06-bird-strike-text.png` + `06-bird-strike-photo.png`
- Card text: *flock of birds / approach / jet aircraft*

**ICAO description:**
This picture shows a bird-strike hazard during take-off. A passenger jet is climbing away from the
runway, and at the same time a flock of birds is flying across its path, with one large bird of prey
very close to the aircraft. There is a high risk that the birds will be ingested into the engines or
hit the windshield. A bird strike at this low altitude is extremely dangerous because it can cause
engine failure right after lift-off. The crew should be ready to report a bird strike and, if an engine
is damaged, declare an emergency and return for landing.

**ICAO Level 5 upgrade:**
This must have been a serious bird-strike hazard during the initial climb. If a bird is ingested, it
could have led to an engine failure right after take-off. The flock crossing the runway may indicate
poor bird control or wildlife near the airfield. From a safety perspective, the main risk is losing
thrust at low altitude, so the crew must have been ready to declare an emergency and return for
landing.

**Repeat-After-Me ICAO Drill:**
1. "This must have been a serious bird-strike hazard."
2. "A bird in the engine could have led to an engine failure."
3. "The flock may indicate poor wildlife control at the airport."
4. "From a safety perspective, the crew should be ready to return and land."

**Mini Speaking Task:**
- Question: Why is this situation dangerous, and what should the crew do?
- Exam-safe answer: A flock of birds is crossing the path of a jet just after take-off. The main risk
  is a bird strike that could have led to an engine failure at low altitude. For safety reasons, if an
  engine is damaged, the safest decision would be to declare an emergency and return for landing.
