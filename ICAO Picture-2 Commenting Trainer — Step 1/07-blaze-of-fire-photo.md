## 07 — Blaze of fire / aircraft on the ground
- Files: `07-blaze-of-fire-text.png` + `07-blaze-of-fire-photo.png`
- Card text: *blaze of fire / AC on the ground*

**ICAO description:**
This is a very dramatic picture of an aircraft on fire on the ground. A passenger jet has made an
emergency landing and a huge blaze of fire has broken out over the rear of the fuselage, with thick
black smoke rising into the sky. The flames are spreading along the top of the aircraft. A situation
like this usually follows a hard landing with a fuel leak, where the fuel has ignited. The crew must
evacuate the passengers as fast as possible using the front slides, away from the flames, while the
fire service fights the fire. Every second counts in this kind of emergency.

**ICAO Level 5 upgrade:**
It must have been a serious fire after landing. It could have been caused by a fuel leak that ignited,
or it might have been caused by an engine fire that spread to the fuselage. The thick black smoke may
indicate that fuel or cabin materials are burning. From a safety perspective, the main risk is that the
fire could have led to a full loss of the aircraft, so an immediate evacuation is essential.

**Repeat-After-Me ICAO Drill:**
1. "It must have been a fire after landing."
2. "It could have been caused by a fuel leak that ignited."
3. "The black smoke may indicate burning fuel."
4. "From a safety perspective, the crew must evacuate immediately."

**Mini Speaking Task:**
- Question: What is happening here, and what is the crew's priority?
- Exam-safe answer: There is a huge blaze of fire on the aircraft after what must have been an
  emergency landing. The main risk is that the fire spreads to the whole fuselage. For safety reasons,
  the crew's priority is to evacuate all passengers immediately using the slides away from the flames.
