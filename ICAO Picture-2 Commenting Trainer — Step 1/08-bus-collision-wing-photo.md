## 08 — Passenger bus / collision / right wing
- Files: `08-bus-collision-wing-text.png` + `08-bus-collision-wing-photo.png`
- Card text: *passnager bus / collision / right wing*

**ICAO description:**
In this picture there has been a collision on the apron between an airport passenger bus and a parked
turboprop aircraft. The bus has driven into the right wing of the aircraft, and the front of the bus is
crushed against the wing. Both the bus and the wing are damaged. The accident happened while the bus
was transporting passengers to or from the terminal. This type of ground collision is usually caused by
the driver misjudging the distance or not following the marked apron routes. The aircraft wing will
need a full inspection for structural damage before it can fly again.

**ICAO Level 5 upgrade:**
It must have been a collision on the apron. It could have been caused by the bus driver misjudging the
distance, or it might have been caused by not following the marked apron routes. The damage to the
right wing may indicate harm to the leading edge or the internal structure. From a safety standpoint,
the wing cannot be ignored, because hidden damage could have led to a structural failure in flight.

**Repeat-After-Me ICAO Drill:**
1. "It must have been a collision on the apron."
2. "It might have been caused by the driver misjudging the distance."
3. "The damage may indicate harm to the wing structure."
4. "From a safety standpoint, the wing must be inspected before flight."

**Mini Speaking Task:**
- Question: What happened on the apron, and what must be done before the aircraft flies?
- Exam-safe answer: A passenger bus must have collided with the right wing of the parked aircraft. It
  may have been caused by the driver getting too close. In terms of flight safety, the defect cannot be
  ignored, so the wing needs a full inspection for structural damage before the aircraft can fly again.
