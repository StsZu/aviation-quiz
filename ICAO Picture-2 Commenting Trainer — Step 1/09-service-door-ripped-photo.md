## 09 — Service door ripped off / gaping hole
- Files: `09-service-door-ripped-text.png` + `09-service-door-ripped-photo.png`
- Card text: *service door / ripped off / gazing hole* (intended: *gaping hole*)

**ICAO description:**
This picture shows an aircraft fuselage with serious structural damage. A service door, near the front
of the cabin, has been ripped off, leaving a large gaping hole in the side of the aircraft. The skin
around the opening is torn and bent outwards. Damage like this can be caused by a sudden
decompression, an explosion, or the door failing in flight. If this happened at altitude, it would
cause a rapid loss of cabin pressure, and the crew would have to descend immediately and put on oxygen.
The aircraft is now on the ground and the damaged area will require major repair.

**ICAO Level 5 upgrade:**
It must have been a structural failure of the service door. It could have been caused by a sudden
decompression, or it might have been caused by a faulty door latch or an explosion. The torn skin
around the opening may indicate that the door blew out under pressure. From a safety perspective, if
this happened at altitude it could have led to a rapid decompression, so the crew would have to descend
and use oxygen immediately.

**Repeat-After-Me ICAO Drill:**
1. "It must have been a structural failure of the door."
2. "It could have been caused by a sudden decompression."
3. "The torn skin may indicate the door blew out under pressure."
4. "From a safety perspective, the crew must descend and use oxygen."

**Mini Speaking Task:**
- Question: What could have caused this damage, and what would happen in flight?
- Exam-safe answer: The service door must have been ripped off, leaving a gaping hole in the fuselage.
  It could have been caused by a faulty latch or a sudden decompression. If it happened at altitude, it
  could have led to a rapid loss of cabin pressure, so the safest decision would be an emergency
  descent with oxygen.
