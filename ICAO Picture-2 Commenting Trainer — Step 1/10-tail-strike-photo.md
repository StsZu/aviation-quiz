## 10 — Aircraft / tail strike
- Files: `10-tail-strike-text.png` + `10-tail-strike-photo.png`
- Card text: *AC / tail strike /*

**ICAO description:**
In this picture I can see a four-engine regional jet during take-off or landing, and it is having a
tail strike. The nose is pitched up very high and the tail of the aircraft is dropping down towards the
runway, so the rear fuselage is about to scrape the ground. A tail strike usually happens when the
aircraft is rotated too quickly or at too high a pitch angle, often because of an incorrect speed or
weight calculation. It can damage the lower rear fuselage and, in serious cases, the pressure bulkhead.
After this kind of event the aircraft must be inspected before further flight.

**ICAO Level 5 upgrade:**
It must have been a tail strike during rotation. It could have been caused by over-rotation, or it
might have been caused by an incorrect speed or weight calculation. The very high pitch angle may
indicate that the crew rotated too early or too fast. From a safety perspective, a tail strike could
have led to damage to the pressure bulkhead, so the aircraft must be inspected before further flight.
