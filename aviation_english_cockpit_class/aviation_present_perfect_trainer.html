STAGE 01

The Core Timeline Idea

Aviation communication depends on immediate clarity. We use the Present Perfect when we report a completed past action that directly creates the present reality. It answers the question: What is the situation right now?

The Equation
PAST ACTION + PRESENT RESULT

If the result is no longer important or the time is past/locked, use Past Simple instead.

Aviation Blueprint
HAVE / HAS + PAST PARTICIPLE

I have checked • The crew has reported

“I have checked the weather”

Meaning: The weather check is complete now and I know the current parameters.

“The crew has reported a problem”

Meaning: Air Traffic Control knows about the problem now. It is currently active.

“We have lost communication”

Meaning: The system is disconnected right now. We cannot hear the tower.

Tactical Rule Check: Present Perfect vs Past Simple

Present Perfect

Result is important and impacts current operations *right now*.

"The weather has changed."
Past Simple

Action was fully complete at a specific time in the past.

"The weather changed at 1430 Zulu."

MODEL SPEECH PATTERN

"The weather has changed before landing. Visibility has decreased, and the runway has become wet. This situation can be dangerous because the aircraft may need a longer landing distance, and the approach may become unstable.

The pilot should check updated weather information, monitor speed and descent rate, and be ready to go around if the approach is not stable. The crew should also listen carefully to ATC instructions and confirm runway conditions.

In my opinion, if the safety margin has become too small, the safest decision is to go around or divert. Safety is more important than completing the landing on the first attempt."
