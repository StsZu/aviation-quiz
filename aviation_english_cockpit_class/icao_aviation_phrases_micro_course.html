SYMPTOMS & EFFECTS

"It can be accompanied by..."

Perfect for demonstrating rich vocabulary by stacking compounding symptoms/effects.

  • • ...accompanied by confusion.
  • • ...accompanied by loud noise.
  • • ...accompanied by vibration & loss of power.
SAFETY SENSITIVITY

"I am concerned about..."

Signals situational awareness and immediate focus on unstable variables in command.

  • • ...concerned about the oil temperature.
  • • ...concerned about cabin pressure drop.
  • • ...concerned about fuel quantities.
DEDUCTIVE SYNTHESIS

"From a safety perspective..."

Formulate high-level conclusions and professional recommendations instantly.

  • • ...perspective, do not continue flight.
  • • ...perspective, declare an emergency.
  • • ...perspective, identify the failure.

DRILL PROMPT 01 Decompression

"What should the crew do if the aircraft experiences explosive decompression?"

Standard Response: "If the aircraft experiences explosive decompression, the situation is very serious. It can be accompanied by loud noise, fog in the cabin, and oxygen mask deployment."

Level 5 Addition: "The crew must don oxygen masks immediately, maintain aircraft control, and start an emergency descent to a safe altitude. From a safety perspective, the main priorities are breathing, aircraft control, and safe descent."

DRILL PROMPT 02 Abnormal Indication

"Explain your actions when you see abnormal oil temperatures during a flight."

Standard Response: "I would check the gauges and tell the tower that the temperature is hot. I would think about changing where I land."

Level 5 Addition: "I would be concerned about this parameter because rising oil temperature may indicate impending engine damage or lubrication failure. From a safety perspective, the crew should maintain control, analyze auxiliary indicators like oil pressure, and prepare for a precautionary landing or divert."

Final Review Summary

Remember: ICAO Level 5 requires precise vocabulary over slang ("don" vs "put on"), detailed technical causes ("debris sucked into engine"), structural linking ("can be accompanied by..."), and prioritizing immediate aviation safety commands.
