03. Common Mistakes Airfield

Recognize and avoid typical non-standard English pitfalls heard during certification exams.

Avoid This Faulty Adjective

"The engine is brokened."

Standard Version:

"The engine is broken."

Key Check: "Broken" is already the past participle functioning as an adjective. There's no need for an extra "-ed".
Avoid This Unnatural Expression

"The tire is without air."

Standard Version:

"The tire is flat / deflated."

Key Check: "Flat" is the natural operational description for non-pressurized pneumatic tires.
Avoid This Informal Phrasing

"The system doesn't work."

Standard Version:

"The system is inoperative."

Key Check: "Inoperative" is formal, technical, and aligned directly with MEL (Minimum Equipment List) syntax.
Avoid This Vague Language

"The aircraft is not good."

Standard Version:

"The aircraft is unserviceable."

Key Check: "Unserviceable" (U/S) indicates the aircraft is not airworthy or ready for flight operations.
Avoid This Basic Verb

"Fuel goes out."

Standard Version:

"Fuel is leaking."

Key Check: "Leaking" explicitly describes fluid or gas escaping unintentionally from structural containers.
Avoid This Informal Descriptor

"The flight is late."

Standard Version:

"The flight is delayed."

Key Check: "Delayed" is the specific operational term used in slot allocations and flight dispatching.
