Passive Voice for Aviation English

Shift focus from who did the action to what happened, what was done, and what must be executed safely. Master professional, objective radio and exam communication.

Core Philosophy

Active Voice (Blame Focus)

Active voice focuses directly on the person/actor. In standard conversation it's common, but in aviation, calling out mistakes directly can sound emotional, direct, or accusatory.

"The pilot made a mistake during approach."

Passive Voice (Procedure Focus)

Passive voice focuses on the action, risk, or result. It sounds objective, safe, and professional, ensuring clarity on what must be resolved.

"A mistake was made during the approach."
The Structural Formula
Subject + BE (auxiliary verb) + Past Participle (V3)

The auxiliary verb "be" changes form based on tense or modal presence (is, was, were, be, being, been), while the main verb always remains in its past participle form.

Operational Passive Patterns

can be done Possibility & Options on the Ground
"The problem can be solved on the ground."
must be done Strict Operational Necessity
"The emergency checklist must be completed."
should be done Best Practice & Safety Guidelines
"The unstable approach should be stabilized."
was / were done Reporting Past Operational Incidents
"Smoke was detected and the flight was delayed."
is being done Current Real-Time Actions (Picture Description)
"The aircraft is being refueled by ground staff."
has been done Completed Events with Present Relevance
"The cabin has been secured for landing."

Active vs. Passive Comparison

Active Input (Direct/Accusatory) Passive Output (Aviation Standard)
The pilot checks the aircraft. The aircraft is checked. зараз перевіряється
The crew completed the checklist. The checklist was completed. був виконаний
They can repair the aircraft. The aircraft can be repaired. можна відремонтувати
The mechanic must inspect the engine. The engine must be inspected. треба оглянути
ATC gave the clearance. The clearance was given. було надано

ICAO Speaking Drills

Emergency Mode PROMPT 1 / 5
What should be done if an emergency arises?

Speak your answer aloud using the passive voice, then reveal the model reply to cross-check.

Avoid simple Active framing: "The pilot must do the checklist and control the plane."
Model ICAO Response (Level 5 Structure)
"In any emergency, the aircraft must be kept under control first. After that, the checklist should be followed and ATC should be informed if time permits."
Using "must be kept", "should be followed", and "should be informed" guarantees structured clarity and maximum score points in grammar evaluation.

Critical Airspace Mistakes to Avoid

Grammatical Navigation Warnings
Incorrect Form (Missing "be")
The aircraft must checked before flight.

Omitted the helping verb 'be'. Modals like 'must', 'should', and 'can' cannot couple directly with V3 participles in passive.

Correct Passive
The aircraft must be checked before flight.

Flawless construction. Clear, correct, and professional.

Incorrect Form (Missing "V3 Participle")
The passengers were evacuate immediately.

Omitted past participle 'evacuated' using root infinitive verb instead. Always use V3.

Correct Passive
The passengers were evacuated immediately.

Maintains logical temporal reporting accuracy in investigations.

Correct Grammar / Unsafe Concept
The aircraft was informed by ATC.

Grammatically correct but contextually awkward. ATC communicates with human pilots/crew, not physical planes. Aviation sense must survive the grammar landing.

Preferred Operational Form
The crew was informed by ATC.

Accurate and logical. Focuses on the personnel processing safety critical directions.
