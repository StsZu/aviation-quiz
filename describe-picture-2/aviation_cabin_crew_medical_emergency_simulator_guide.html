Cardiac & Stroke Emergencies

A heart attack severely damages the heart and demands rapid expert intervention. If the heart stops fully (cardiac arrest), crew must immediately start CPR or locate and utilize the onboard AED. Simultaneously, rapid brain care is crucial for a stroke to minimize permanent neurological damage.

Aviation standard policy "Every 2 minutes counts"

Obstruction & Allergies

Food ingestion can trigger serious cabin crises: airway obstructions causing a passenger to choke requires the immediate application of the physical Heimlich maneuver. For severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) from animal dander, chemicals, or food allergens, locate and administer the EpiPen®.

Physical & chemical responses "Administer immediately"

Mandatory Cabin Safety

A certified onboard biohazard kit is strictly mandatory to safely manage infectious elements and hazardous spills. Additionally, every flight attendant must maintain a current, active certification in CPR to provide critical support when external physicians are absent.

Aviation authority regulations "Strict compliance"

CARDIAC ARREST

Severe Heart Damage / Cessation

If the heart's pumping actions stop entirely, immediate professional intervention is non-negotiable. Administer chest compressions.

MANDATORY PROTOCOLS
Primary Tool: AED & CPR
Crew Status: CPR Certification Required
⚠️ If the passenger suffers from a heart attack, prepare for transition to cardiac arrest and notify the flight deck immediately.
STROKE RELIEF

Neurological Brain Event

Arises from a sudden blockage or hemorrhage of blood vessels in the brain. Fast proper treatment limits permanent damage to speech or motor skills.

MANDATORY PROTOCOLS
Primary Action: Immediate Professional Intervention
Cabin Duty: Rapid Flight Diversion Coordination
ANAPHYLAXIS & ALLERGIES

Allergen Overreaction

Severe hypersensitivity triggered by animal exposure, heavy chemicals, or food allergens. If airways begin to restrict, actions must be prompt.

MANDATORY PROTOCOLS
Primary Tool: EpiPen® (Adrenaline Injector)
Risk Scale: Varies from Mild Rash to Suffocation
CHOKING & OBSTRUCTION

Airway Obstruction

Passenger is unable to speak or breathe due to food blockage in the windpipe. Acting immediately saves lives.

MANDATORY PROTOCOLS
Primary Maneuver: Heimlich Maneuver
Action Purpose: Dislodge and eject food blockage
HAZARDOUS BIOHAZARD SPILL

Biological Fluid Contamination

Biological fluids, chemical toxins, or dangerous spills in the galley or passenger aisles. Mandatory cleanup protocols protect health.

MANDATORY PROTOCOLS
Primary Tool: Onboard Biohazard Kit
Cabin Standard: Mandatory Equipment Onboard

Critical Distinctions & Pitfalls to Avoid

Prevent clinical or terminology errors during severe emergencies.

Concept Group Incorrect Action / Term Correct Protocol / Term Safety Rationale
Sudden Cessation vs Damage "Passenger suffered cardiac arrest, we must wait for doctors to arrive" Immediately begin CPR or fetch AED defibrillator Cardiac arrest stops circulation. Waiting for landing or doctors results in rapid brain death.
Choking vs Anaphylaxis Using an EpiPen to treat airway food blockages Perform abdominal Heimlich maneuver thrusts Adrenaline cannot bypass mechanical blockages. It is strictly for systemic severe allergic swellings.
Hazard Management Using normal seat towels to scrub biological spills Deploy onboard Biohazard safety kit components Prevents transmission of blood-borne pathogens across crew and subsequent cabin occupants.
