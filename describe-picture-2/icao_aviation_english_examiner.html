Critical Operations Glossary

Crabbed - Nose angled towards wind vector to maintain straight runway centerline track.
Balked Landing - An aborted landing approach at very low level, leading directly to a go-around.
Nacelle / Cowling - The external housing or aerodynamic skin surrounding an aircraft's turbine engine.
Apron / Ramp - Defined operational area on an airfield intended to accommodate aircraft for loading and turnaround.
Overpass / Elevated Highway - A concrete bridge structure carrying a public roadway over other infrastructure.

ICAO Exam Trap Analyzer

HIGH INCIDENCE PILOT ERROR
"The engine must hit by baggage tug during turnaround."
EXAM COMPLIANT PATTERN
"The engine cowling must have been struck by a baggage tug on the ground."

Why? Aviation examiners require the passive past perfect modal construct ("must + have + been + V3") to accurately assess logical historical deductions where the direct action was not directly recorded but deduced.
