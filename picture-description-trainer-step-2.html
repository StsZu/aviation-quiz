Step 2 of the picture-description method

ICAO Picture Description Trainer — Step 2: Detailed Description

After your opening topic statement (Step 1), the examiner expects a full detailed description of the scene. This trainer teaches you to study, shadow and reproduce the model descriptions over ten new aviation incidents — moving from a clear Level 4 description to a Level 5 upgrade with possibility, deduction and safety language.

  • describe what is happening with present continuous / present perfect;
  • use precise aviation vocabulary and cause-and-effect links;
  • upgrade to Level 5 deduction — it must have been…, could have been caused by…, may indicate…;
  • add safety framing — from a safety perspective…, the main risk is…;
  • reproduce the whole description from memory and answer an examiner question.

Daily Training Mode

A simple 20-minute routine built around reproducing the texts.

  1. 5 minutes — read one detailed description and listen to its audio.
  2. 5 minutes — shadow the audio: repeat in sync, copying rhythm and stress.
  3. 5 minutes — reconstruct the description from the keywords only, without reading.
  4. 5 minutes — answer the mini speaking task and add the Level 5 deduction line.

Level 5 Speaking Templates

Reusable deduction + safety frames drawn from the recurring phrases in these scenes. Slot your own cause and risk into the blanks.

1 — Deduction of cause

It must have been a [type of incident]. It could have been caused by [cause A], or it might have been caused by [cause B].

2 — Reading the evidence

The [visible damage] may indicate [hidden problem]. The position of the [object] may indicate that [what probably happened].

3 — Safety framing

From a safety perspective, the main risk is [risk], because [hazard] could have led to [worse outcome].

4 — Safest decision

So the safest decision would be to [action] — for example, to evacuate the passengers, go around, or inspect the aircraft before further flight.
