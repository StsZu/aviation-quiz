SCAN Method

The Pilot's Picture Scan — describe any aviation picture with one small loop.

One loop. Four zones. Full answer.

A pilot doesn't memorise the instruments — he scans them in a loop. You don't memorise a description structure — you scan the picture with one loop of four steps:

S
See
What can I see?
“I can see…”
C
Call
What is it called?
“It looks like a…”
A
Assume
What may have happened?
“It may have…”
N
Next
What happens next?
“The crew would…”

Run this loop over four zones of the picture. Four zones × four sentences = a full ICAO-style answer, but your memory only holds one small loop.

The Assume step uses a “traffic light” of certainty:

might have / may have — a weak guess
could have / probably — a medium guess
must have… because I can see… — a strong guess with evidence

Stuck? Press the red SOS button. Rescue phrases are not a weakness — paraphrasing a forgotten word is a scored ICAO vocabulary skill.

Trainer — Runway Excursion

Pick a zone (tap the picture or a button), then walk the S-C-A-N loop. Learn mode shows the model sentence. Drill mode hides it — say your own sentence first, then check.

Evidence drill — fact → guess

Modal verbs are not grammar — they are bricks. Read the visible fact, then say a guess with the modal shown. Strong guesses add evidence: …because I can see…

Speak — record your full answer

Look at the picture above, press Start and describe it aloud (aim for 60–90 seconds, all four zones). The trainer records you, transcribes your speech and measures your ICAO metrics automatically.

Universal skeleton — works on any picture

Use this as a printed support in weeks 4–5, then turn it over. Final chant: I see → I name → I prove → I guess → I act → I conclude.

This picture shows an aviation incident.

I can see a [type of aircraft] near / on / above / beside [place].

The main problem seems to be [problem].
I can see this because [visible evidence].

In the background, there are [people / vehicles / equipment].

The incident may have been caused by [weather / technical problem /
human factor / runway condition].

The crew would need to [action].
ATC would need to [action].

From a safety perspective, the main risk is [risk].

In my opinion, this situation shows that [safety lesson].
