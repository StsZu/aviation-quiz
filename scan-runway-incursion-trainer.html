The picture

One aircraft is on final approach with its landing lights on, while a large wide-body aircraft is still on the active runway — a possible runway incursion.

The method

One loop. Four zones. Full answer.

You don't memorise a structure — you scan the picture with one small loop of four steps, run over four zones.

Four zones: Plane · People · Place · Panorama. Four zones × four sentences = a full ICAO-style answer.

The Assume step uses a "traffic light" of certainty:

might have / may have — a weak guess
could have / probably — a medium guess
must have… because I can see… — a strong guess with evidence

ICAO picture skeleton

ICAO Picture Skeleton — Runway Incursion / Traffic Conflict

1. General situation

  • busy runway / active runway
  • one aircraft landing / on final approach
  • another aircraft on runway / crossing or entering runway
  • possible runway incursion
  • high-risk situation

2. What I can see

  • large wide-body aircraft on the runway
  • Delta / SkyTeam livery
  • another aircraft approaching from behind / above
  • landing lights on
  • control tower in the background
  • runway markings and edge lights
  • evening / sunset conditions

3. What may be happening

  • aircraft on final approach
  • aircraft on runway not clear yet
  • possible clearance misunderstanding
  • possible late runway crossing
  • possible ATC coordination problem
  • possible go-around situation

4. Possible causes

  • communication breakdown
  • incorrect readback / misunderstood clearance
  • ATC sequencing problem
  • aircraft entered runway without clearance
  • slow runway vacating
  • reduced situational awareness
  • runway layout complexity

5. Main risks

  • collision on runway
  • landing aircraft cannot stop in time
  • late go-around
  • wake turbulence / jet blast
  • loss of separation
  • serious accident if both continue

6. What the pilots should do

Landing aircraft:

  • monitor runway condition
  • if runway occupied — go around
  • inform ATC
  • maintain safe altitude and separation

Aircraft on runway:

  • stop if instructed
  • vacate runway immediately if safe
  • confirm instructions with ATC
  • avoid crossing active runway without clearance

ATC:

  • cancel landing clearance
  • instruct go-around
  • stop aircraft on runway
  • give clear, short instructions
  • prevent further conflict

7. Safety conclusion

  • runway must be clear before landing
  • clear communication is critical
  • if there is any doubt — clarify
  • go-around is the safest option
  • safety more important than continuing the approach

Short speaking frame

Short speaking frame

Use this order:

  1. I can see → aircraft landing + aircraft on runway
  2. It may be → runway incursion / traffic conflict
  3. Possible causes → misunderstanding / ATC / clearance issue
  4. Main risk → collision / loss of separation
  5. Action → go-around / vacate runway / clarify with ATC
  6. Conclusion → safety first, runway must be clear
